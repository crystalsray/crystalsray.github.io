As you have seen, the bounding exercise can provide interesting insights. There are several additional applications that you may be able to utilize to refine your bounds estimates and calculate sharper ranges of treatment control impacts. Please find relevant references below.



Frangakis, C. E. and D. B. Rubin (2002). Principal Stratification in Causal Inference. Biometrics, 58 (1), 21–29.

Page, L., A. Feller, T. Grindal, L. Miratrix, and M.-A. Somers (2015, July). Principal stratification: A tool for understanding variation in program effects across endogenous subgroups . American Journal of Evaluation 36 (4), 514–531.

Feller, A., T. Grindal, L. Miratrix, and L. C. Page (2016). Compared to what? Variation in the impacts of early childhood education by alternative care type. The Annals of Applied Statistics 10 (3), 1245–1285.

Miratrix, L, J. Furey, A. Feller, T. Grindal, L. Page (2017). Bounding, an accessible method for estimating principal causal effects, examined and explained. Working Paper.

Researchers unfamiliar with the approach are encouraged to consult Page et al 2015 before using this tool.
