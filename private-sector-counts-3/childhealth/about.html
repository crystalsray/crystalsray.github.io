About this Tool

Health Source Counts is intended to assist donors and program implementers with designing country programs that support USAID efforts to end preventable child deaths. Understanding whether and where caregivers seek advice or treatment for their sick children is critical to inform successful programs. As users of the tool will find, caregivers go to both public and private sector providers, the proportion of caregivers going to either one sector or the other varies considerably by country. The tool uses DHS data for three common childhood illnesses: acute respiratory infection (ARI), fever, and diarrhea.

A total market approach

The tool shows the contribution of public and private sector providers to the treatment of three treatable childhood illnesses in 24 of USAID’s maternal and child survival priority countries. Traditionally, donors and program implementers have focused interventions on the public sector. Local private providers also are an important source of care and often the first source of care for these illnesses. With easy-to-access data on where caregivers obtain treatment or advice, implementers can design programs that access to quality services throughout the health system.

For information on a total market approach to child health, visit shopsplusproject.org/tma

Relevance of Health Source Counts

Health Source Counts is designed to help users gain a better understanding of the contribution of the public and private sector to common, treatable childhood illnesses that account for a high proportion of child deaths. This tool allows users to answer the following questions by country and region:

  • How prevalent is ARI, fever, and diarrhea?
  • How frequently is out-of-home care sought for children with these illnesses?
  • Among those who seek out-of-home care, what are the sources (public, private, or other)?

The tool answers these questions by displaying the data in color-coded rows. Details on the mix of treatment source are displayed by hovering the cursor over a cell. Users can download the data into an Excel spreadsheet.

SHOPS Plus Project

Sustaining Health Outcomes through the Private Sector (SHOPS) Plus project is USAID’s flagship initiative in private sector health. The project seeks to harness the full potential of the private sector and catalyze public-private engagement to improve health outcomes in family planning, HIV, and maternal and child health. By increasing the participation and effectiveness of the private health sector, SHOPS Plus improves the equity and quality of the total health system.
